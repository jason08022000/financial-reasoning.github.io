Introduction

The growing complexity of financial mathematics demands robust educational tools that can enhance learners' problem-solving and reasoning skills. In this context, multimodal learning, which integrates various modes of information such as text, visuals, and interactive elements, has gained significant attention. However, learners often struggle with self-reflection and tracking their errors, which are critical for improving their understanding and performance. This paper introduces an innovative approach that integrates a reflection module and an error log system into multimodal financial math education, aiming to enhance students‚Äô reasoning skills by encouraging deeper self-assessment and targeted correction of mistakes.
